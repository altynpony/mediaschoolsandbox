NOV 2018

First Demolition Orders in Samarkand

City mayor orders demolition of 500 private houses affecting 6,000 residents without consultation.

  • Decision taken without consultation with affected residents
  • Project aims to "modernise" Samarkand city center
  • Houses located in UNESCO buffer zone, near protected monuments
  • Plan to replace homes with multi-story hotels and residential buildings
  • Khodja Akhrar neighborhood residents given only 3 days notice
Impact: 6,000 residents at risk
2019

UN Special Rapporteurs Raise Alarm

UN experts express serious concern about forced evictions and housing demolitions in Samarkand UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Special Rapporteurs on housing and cultural rights issue formal communication
  • Highlight violations of international human rights law
  • Note lack of consultation, inadequate compensation, no housing alternatives
  • Warn about threats to cultural heritage and community ties
  • Call for moratorium on demolitions
Status: Warning Ignored
2018-2024

40,000 Threatened in Tashkent Urban Renewal

Massive urban regeneration project evicts at least 10,000 people, threatens 30,000 more across the capital.

  • At least 10,000 already evicted from city center homes
  • Further 30,000 under threat as project continues
  • Historic mahallas destroyed, replaced by "Tashkent City" business center
  • Compensation much lower than property values
  • Temporary housing far from work, schools, community ties
  • 300 billion UZS in compensation still unpaid by July 2019
Pattern: Systemic violations across regions
MAY 2025

🚨 Al-Maturidi Evictions: 1,200 People Displaced

Entire neighborhood near al-Maturidi mausoleum demolished in weeks. Multoni Roma community loses homes of generations.

  • Over 1,200 people forcibly evicted from UNESCO protected zone
  • Primarily affects Multoni minority, a local Roma community
  • Community lived in area for generations with deep cultural connections
  • No alternatives explored, no resettlement plan agreed upon
  • Expropriation under severe police and judicial pressure
  • Lawyer defending homeowner threatened and detained
  • Utilities disconnected to force residents out
  • Compensation insufficient to buy similar housing in city center
Human Rights Crisis: Targeting of ethnic minority
OCT 2025

UN Condemns, Activists Silenced

UN experts condemn forced evictions. Government responds by blocking phones and intimidating activists ahead of UNESCO conference.

  • UN experts express grave concern about Samarkand evictions and criticize UNESCO for inaction
  • Government launches crackdown on activists and bloggers
  • Abdurahmon Tashanov (Ezgulik director): Phone blocked 4 days
  • Xudoyberdi Zominiy: Questioned by counter-terrorism unit
  • Zafar Xandamov: Internet and mobile completely blocked
  • Multiple activists report similar intimidation tactics
Repression: Restrictions on freedom of expression during UNESCO visit
NOV 2025

UNESCO Conference Begins in Samarkand

43rd General Conference held in Samarkand—first time in 40 years outside Paris. President speaks on heritage protection.

  • First UNESCO General Conference outside Paris in 40 years
  • President Mirziyoyev proposes UNESCO Academy for Women's Leadership
  • Calls for resolution on preserving cultural heritage
  • Government celebrates hosting "prestigious" event
  • No acknowledgment of 1,200 people evicted months earlier
  • Activists remain silenced, phones still blocked
  • UNESCO fails to address human rights violations in host city
